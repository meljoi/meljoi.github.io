Manhattan

It isn't named the city that never sleeps for nothing! Mahattan is known for its lights in Times Square, it's many resturants and various tourist attractions such as Central Park, the Met, Rocefeller Center just to name a few.

Bronx

Don't sleep on the Bronx! This borough of New York City is known for Yankee Stadium, the home field of the New York Yankees baseball team. Also, The Bronx Zoo which houses hundreds of species of animals. Nearby, the sprawling New York Botanical Garden features a landmark greenhouse with rainforest and beautiful greenery.

Queens

Tennis anyone! The borough of Queens is home to Flushing Meadows Corona Park, with the Unisphere, a 12-story 1964 World's Fair globe sculpture (famous scene in the first Men In Black movie), which hosts the annual U.S. Open tennis tournament. It's also home to Citi Field, the stadium of pro baseball team, the Mets.

Brooklyn

Brooklyn in the house! This is the most populous borough of New York City and home to Coney Island. There you could ride the famous Cyclone coaster and take part in a hot dog eating contest at Nathans Famous Franks. Its also home to prospect park and the hipster-chic neighborhood of Williamsburg.
